## Pattern: Low-cap spike clusters
Tokens under $1M mcap on Base tend to spike in clusters — when one starts moving, 2-3 others in the same narrative move within 30-60 minutes. Watch for correlated buys across small caps. Observed in cycles 12, 19, 31, 44.

## Pattern: Aerodrome vs Uniswap timing
Aerodrome spikes tend to lead Uniswap by ~15 minutes on the same token. If I see a spike on Aerodrome, check Uniswap pair for the same address — usually catches the move earlier. First noticed cycle 8, confirmed cycles 22, 37.

## Pattern: Buy/sell ratio as confirmation
Spike ratio alone can be misleading (wash trading). A spike with buys1h/sells1h > 4:1 is much higher confidence than spike ratio alone. Tokens with high ratio but balanced buys/sells are usually bot activity. Refined in cycles 28-35.

## Pattern: Ghost tokens (no social presence)
Several spikes have been from tokens with 0 Google results and 0 Twitter presence. These pump fast (15-30 min) and dump completely. Not worth flagging — exit liquidity traps. Learned cycles 15, 23, 41.

## Pattern: Weekend vs weekday behavior
Friday 18:00-22:00 UTC and Saturday activity is 2.3x higher than Tuesday-Wednesday. Spike count peaks Friday evening. Adjust alert thresholds down on weekends. Tracking since cycle 20.

## Pattern: Spike decay rate
High spike ratios (>10x) rarely sustain more than 2-3 cycles (1-1.5 hours). If I flag a token in cycle N and it's still spiking in cycle N+2, it's a genuine move. If it disappears, it was a flush. Filed under: temporal validation.
